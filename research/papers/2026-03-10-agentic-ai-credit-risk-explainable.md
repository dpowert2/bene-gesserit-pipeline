# Agentic AI for Autonomous, Explainable, and Real-Time Credit Risk Decision-Making

**Authors:** Not specified in abstract (arXiv preprint)
**Published:** December 22, 2025 (arXiv:2601.00818)

**Key finding:** The paper presents a multi-agent framework for credit risk assessment where agents operate with reinforcement learning, natural language reasoning, explainable AI modules, and real-time data pipelines — enabling fully autonomous yet auditable credit decisions without continuous human supervision.

**Thesis relevance:** Directly validates the Personal Agent and Compliance gap categories — autonomous agents making high-stakes financial decisions require exactly the explainability and audit-trail infrastructure that the thesis identifies as critical for regulated deployment. The reinforcement learning + NLP reasoning combination also has implications for tax optimisation and portfolio agents.
