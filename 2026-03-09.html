Agentic Artificial Intelligence in Finance: A Comprehensive Survey

Key finding: Comprehensive survey of agentic AI applications across finance, covering autonomous trading, wealth management, risk management, and compliance. Maps the landscape of how AI agents are transforming financial services from chatbot-era to fully autonomous operational agents.

Provides the academic foundation for the entire Bene Gesserit thesis — surveying exactly the transition from passive AI to agentic AI in finance that creates the demand for regulated infrastructure partners.

The Agentic Regulator: Risks for AI in Finance and a Proposed Agent-based Framework for Governance

Key finding: Proposes an agent-based framework for AI governance in financial services, addressing the risks of agentic AI deployment. Argues that regulators themselves should adopt agentic approaches to keep pace with AI-driven financial services.

Directly validates the Compliance gap and regulatory urgency. If regulators adopt agentic approaches, the compliance infrastructure for AI agents becomes even more critical.

Agentic AI Systems Applied to Tasks in Financial Services: Modeling and Model Risk Management Crews

Key finding: Explores agentic crew workflows with human-in-the-loop modules for financial modeling and model risk management. Demonstrates that multi-agent collaboration can handle complex regulated financial tasks with specialized roles.

Directly validates the Agent Orchestration gap — multi-agent crews with defined roles can execute complex financial modeling workflows. Also highlights the importance of human-in-the-loop for regulated contexts.
