Agent Authentication & Security Reinforces

Ramp + Visa Deploy AI Agents for Corporate Bill Pay via Trusted Agent Protocol

Ramp and Visa announced an expanded multi-year partnership on March 31, integrating Visa's Trusted Agent Protocol into Ramp's corporate spend platform for 50,000+ customers. The protocol — built with Cloudflare — establishes a framework for AI agents to autonomously search, compare, and execute corporate bill payments while cryptographically distinguishing trusted agents from malicious bots. This is card-network-grade identity and authorization infrastructure being rolled out at scale for autonomous finance, validating that agent credentialing is now a production requirement, not a research problem.

Big Tech Reinforces

Mastercard Completes First Live Agentic Transaction in Hong Kong with HSBC and DBS

Mastercard executed its first live agentic transaction in Hong Kong — an AI agent booking and paying for a rideshare via Mastercard Agent Pay, using tokenised credentials and Payment Passkeys — in partnership with HSBC, DBS, Citi HK, Hang Seng, Standard Chartered HK, and Mox Bank. This follows live deployments in Australia, the U.S., and India. The signal here is structural: six major regulated banks in one city executed a live autonomous payment in a production environment, months ahead of most fintech pilot timelines.

Agent Orchestration New Signal

Meridian AI Raises $17M Seed for Agentic Financial Modeling IDE

Meridian emerged from stealth with $17M led by a16z and QED Investors to build a Cursor-style IDE for complex financial modeling — explicitly replacing Excel with an auditable, agent-driven workspace. The team spans Scale AI, Anthropic, and Goldman Sachs alumni. The company signed $5M in contracts in December 2025 alone. This signals that the FP&A and financial modeling layer is being captured by AI-native tooling that prioritises auditability and traceability — exactly the properties regulators will eventually mandate for agent-driven finance decisions.

Compliance New Signal

Lema AI Raises $24M Series A to Bring Agentic AI to Third-Party Vendor Risk

Lema AI emerged from stealth with a $24M Series A led by Team8 (with Salesforce Ventures) to replace static vendor compliance questionnaires with continuous, agentic monitoring of third-party attack surfaces. Customers span financial services and healthcare Fortune 500s. Under DORA and updated OCC third-party guidance, this is exactly the kind of always-on vendor oversight regulators are beginning to expect — making Lema a direct compliance infrastructure play for your regulated environment.

Agent Authentication & Security Reinforces

IBM, Auth0, and Yubico Form Human-in-the-Loop Framework for High-Stakes AI Agent Actions

Presented at RSAC 2026 on March 24, IBM, Auth0, and Yubico introduced a production-ready Human-in-the-Loop authorization framework: IBM's WatsonX agents propose actions, Auth0 triggers CIBA-standard approval requests, and YubiKey taps provide cryptographic proof that a specific human physically authorised the decision. The model targets large financial transfers, production deployments, and sensitive data access. This is the enterprise blueprint for "supervised autonomy" — agents that act freely on routine tasks but require non-repudiable human sign-off for regulated actions.

Regulation Reinforces

SEC 2026 Division of Examinations Priorities: AI Governance Now a Cross-Cutting Risk Area

The SEC's 2026 exam priorities explicitly flag automated investment tools, algorithmic models, and AI systems as primary examination targets — asking whether AI capability representations are accurate, whether algorithms produce advice consistent with investor strategies, and whether firms have governance frameworks covering vendor oversight, hallucination risk, and AML automation. AI has displaced crypto as the dominant exam risk category. For any firm building or partnering with AI agent infrastructure in financial services, exam readiness around AI governance is now a board-level priority.

Compliance Market Signal

Agentic AI Is Closing the Financial Crime Gap

Fintech.Global's April 1 analysis argues that the real-time, always-on nature of agentic AI — observing, reasoning, and acting continuously — is fundamentally better suited to financial crime prevention than legacy rule-based systems. Agentic AML systems can monitor contextual behavioural patterns across accounts in real time, not just flag static rule violations. This is a market-signal that financial crime compliance is emerging as one of the highest-conviction verticals for agentic AI deployment — with Steward, Bretton AI, Vivox AI, and Sphinx already in the space, competitive intensity is accelerating.
