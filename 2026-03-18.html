Regulation Reinforces

US Treasury Publishes Financial Services AI Risk Management Framework

The US Department of the Treasury released the Financial Services AI Risk Management Framework (FS AI RMF), a sector-specific extension of the NIST framework defining 230 control objectives across four governance functions: govern, map, measure, and manage. Published alongside a shared AI Lexicon with common definitions for 230+ AI concepts, the framework was developed via a public-private partnership between Treasury's FBIIC and the Financial Services Sector Coordinating Council. This is the clearest US regulatory signal yet that agentic AI deployments inside banks will need formal governance architecture — precisely the layer your strategy is building.

Agentic AI Rewiring Insurance Claims: Sedgwick Reports 30%+ Efficiency Gains

A March 17 industry analysis confirms agentic AI is shifting from assistant to actor in insurance claims — systems now analyse First Notice of Loss, identify exposure, and propose concrete next steps without human initiation. Sedgwick's Sidekick Agent (built with Microsoft) is delivering 30%+ efficiency gains; Travelers has deployed a fully agentic inbound voice service for auto claims. The universal caveat across the industry is that human-in-the-loop oversight remains non-negotiable — validating that you need a compliant, auditable infrastructure layer, not just raw agent capability.

Regulation Reinforces

EU AI Act High-Risk Rules for Financial AI Taking Effect August 2026

By August 2, 2026, EU-facing financial institutions must comply with transparency requirements and safeguards for high-risk AI systems — specifically including creditworthiness assessment and credit scoring. Legal analysis from BCLP confirms no direct contradictions between the AI Act and existing EU banking regulation, but the co-existence of multiple supervising authorities creates significant compliance overhead. This is a hard deadline that makes the "regulated infrastructure layer for agentic AI" conversation urgent rather than theoretical for any institution with EU exposure.
