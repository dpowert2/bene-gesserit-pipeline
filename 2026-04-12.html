FCA Eyes Rule Change as Agentic AI Comes to Payments

The FCA is evaluating "whether change or development of regulation is needed to support agentic AI payments." AI Safety & Guardrails Reinforces

FINRA 2026 Oversight Report Signals Supervisory Reckoning for Autonomous AI

FINRA's 2026 Annual Regulatory Oversight Report explicitly designates agentic AI — systems that autonomously execute multi-step tasks — as an emerging supervisory risk. Firms deploying autonomous agents must maintain controls compliant with FINRA Rule 3110 (Supervision) and Rule 4370 (Business Continuity), define "human in the loop" oversight protocols, and implement guardrails restricting agent behaviors. The report makes clear that once an AI system can act rather than merely generate content, a firm's supervisory, books-and-records, and governance obligations shift materially. Every broker-dealer racing to deploy finance agents now has a compliance checklist that creates demand for your infrastructure.

Agentic AI Foundation (AAIF) Formed Under Linux Foundation — MCP Goes Neutral

In December 2025, Anthropic donated the Model Context Protocol (MCP) to the newly formed Agentic AI Foundation (AAIF) under the Linux Foundation, co-founded with Block and OpenAI. Platinum members include AWS, Anthropic, Block, Bloomberg, Cloudflare, Google, Microsoft, and OpenAI. MCP — now a neutral open standard — becomes the connection layer between AI models and tools. Bloomberg's platinum membership is a direct signal that financial services data infrastructure is in scope. Open-standards governance of the agent protocol layer is strategically positive: it confirms MCP as the interoperability surface your infrastructure needs to support, and creates a formal venue where financial services requirements can be surfaced to the standards body.
