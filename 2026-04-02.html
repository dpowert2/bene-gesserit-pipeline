Regulation Challenges

Agentic commerce is outpacing regulation designed for humans

The Center for Data Innovation argues that regulation written for human actors — KYC, AML, fiduciary suitability, disclosure requirements — creates significant friction for agentic commerce, and that the resulting uncertainty could slow adoption or push agents toward less-regulated channels. This is a genuine challenge to the thesis: if human-designed regulation permanently handicaps agentic finance, the regulated infrastructure layer loses its advantage. The counter-read is that this friction is exactly what creates demand for a compliant intermediary — a trusted operating layer that absorbs the regulatory complexity so that agents don't have to.
