## One-liner
InFlow is a payments platform purpose-built for agentic commerce, enabling AI agents to autonomously onboard to third-party services, execute purchases within policy-approved guardrails, and maintain compliance with organizational identity and payment policies — without human intervention.

## FinServ Signal
InFlow targets the exact compliance and identity gap that financial institutions will face as agentic AI proliferates: how does a regulated firm ensure an autonomous agent only spends within approved limits, on approved merchants, with a full audit trail? The platform's three pillars — autonomous onboarding, contextual payments, and policy compliance — map directly to what treasury, procurement, and compliance teams at banks need. The addressable market includes the McKinsey-projected $1 trillion in agentic US retail commerce by 2030.

## Team
San Francisco-based team; founding details and specific investor backing not yet disclosed publicly. Launched December 4, 2025. Early-stage but one of the first pure-play agent payments companies to productize the onboarding + compliance stack.

## Thesis
InFlow is tackling the least-solved problem in agentic finance: how agents authenticate and transact with third-party services at the moment of action, not just at setup. The policy compliance and identity layer — where agents use pre-approved organizational credentials — is the same problem every bank's procurement and treasury team will face when they deploy AI agents at scale. This gives you a potential partnership opportunity in the agent onboarding and compliance layer, or a signal of where the regulated operating system needs to connect.
