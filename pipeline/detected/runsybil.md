## One-liner
RunSybil deploys AI agents — led by its flagship agent "Sybil" — to conduct fully autonomous, black-box penetration testing against live production applications, finding and documenting real vulnerabilities without human involvement.

## FinServ Signal
RunSybil already counts "major financial institutions" among its early customers, alongside high-growth tech firms like Cursor, Notion, and Thinking Machines Lab. Its black-box approach (no source code required) is particularly suited to financial services, where vendors rarely share internal code with security testers. Its angel roster — including Nikesh Arora (Chairman/CEO, Palo Alto Networks) and founders from Stripe — signals credibility with regulated-industry security buyers.

## Team
Founded by OpenAI's first security hire. The $40M seed round was led by Khosla Ventures, with participation from S32, the Anthology Fund (Anthropic/Menlo Ventures), Conviction, and Elad Gil. Angels include Nikesh Arora, Amit Agarwal, Jeff Dean, and senior alumni from OpenAI, Palo Alto Networks, Stripe, and Google.

## Thesis
Financial services firms face a compounding security problem: they must secure both traditional infrastructure and a rapidly growing fleet of AI agents — each of which is a new attack surface. RunSybil's continuous, autonomous pen testing closes the window between compliance audits and real-world exposure. As you build the regulated infrastructure layer that AI agents connect to, RunSybil is a natural security validation partner — and its Stripe/payments investor DNA suggests openness to financial-services-first product thinking.
