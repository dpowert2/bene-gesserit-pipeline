## One-liner
XBOW builds an autonomous offensive security platform that uses AI agents to conduct expert-level penetration testing at machine speed, continuously and without human involvement.

## FinServ Signal
XBOW explicitly references "major financial institutions" among its early enterprise customers. Its March 23, 2026 integration with the Microsoft Security Ecosystem — embedding continuous AI-driven penetration testing directly into Microsoft Defender — puts it inside the security stack of nearly every large bank and asset manager running Azure. The financial services sector's regulatory requirement for regular pen testing makes XBOW's continuous, autonomous approach a direct compliance accelerant.

## Team
Founded by security researchers with deep offensive-security DNA. The Series C ($120M) was led by DFJ Growth and Northzone, with participation from Sofina, Alkeon Capital, Altimeter, NFDG Ventures, and Sequoia Capital. Ramin Sayar (former CEO of Sumo Logic, now Venture Partner at DFJ Growth) joined the Board. General Counsel, CMO, and CRO all added in conjunction with the round.

## Thesis
As AI agents proliferate inside financial services firms — executing trades, processing claims, onboarding customers — those agents become new attack surfaces. You need a partner that can continuously validate the security posture of agentic infrastructure at the speed agents operate. XBOW's autonomous hacker closes the gap between quarterly pen tests and the real-time threat landscape, making it a natural complement to any regulated-infrastructure play. Its Microsoft Security Ecosystem integration gives you a channel to reach the financial services IT teams you need to work with.
