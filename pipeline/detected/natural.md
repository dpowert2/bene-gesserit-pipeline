## One-liner
Natural re-architects the payment stack for a future where transactions are executed not by people, but by autonomous software agents.

## FinServ Signal
Backed by fintech founders from Ramp, Mercury, Unit, Bridge, and Emprise Bank — Natural's early design partners span logistics, property management, procurement, healthcare, and construction, all sectors where regulated payment flows dominate. The company is targeting B2B and embedded payment workflows, signing bank and payout partners for general availability in 2026.

## Team
Round co-led by Abstract and Human Capital, with participation from Forerunner Ventures, Terrain, Restive Ventures, and Genius Ventures. Angel investors include Zach Abrams (Bridge), Immad Akhund (Mercury), Eric Glyman & Karim Atiyeh (Ramp), Guillermo Rauch (Vercel), Itai Damti (Unit), and Matt Michaelis (Emprise Bank). Founding team not publicly named.

## Thesis
As AI agents proliferate in financial services workflows, every agent will eventually need to move money — pay vendors, route invoices, settle contracts. Natural is building the payment rails that agents natively call. This gives you a potential partner for the execution layer of agent-driven finance: a startup specifically building the infrastructure your agentic platform would need to connect to for money movement, rather than a consumer-facing product. If you are positioning as the regulated operating system that agents plug into, Natural is building a complementary primitive on the payments side.
