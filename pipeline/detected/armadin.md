# Armadin

**URL:** https://armadin.com/
**Gap:** AI Safety & Guardrails
**Stage:** Seed / Series A
**Geo:** United States (San Francisco)
**Score:** 68

## One-liner
Armadin builds autonomous AI cybersecurity agents that continuously simulate attacks and respond to threats without a human in the loop, designed to give defenders an "agentic army" to counter AI-powered adversaries.

## FinServ Signal
Armadin is already working with Fortune 100 companies and hired 60+ employees in its first six months. CIA venture arm In-Q-Tel is a backer, signalling national-security-grade trust requirements — the same bar that financial regulators increasingly apply to critical infrastructure. The autonomous red-teaming model (adversarial agent vs. defensive agent) maps directly to the AI-driven fraud and model-attack scenarios that banks and insurers are stress-testing under DORA and the EU AI Act.

## Team
Founded by Kevin Mandia (founder of Mandiant, sold to Google for $5.4B in 2022), Travis Lanham (former Google Cloud Security principal engineer), Evan Peña (former Mandiant exec), and David Slater (former Google SecOps engineer). $189.9M raised in combined seed and Series A led by Accel, with GV, Kleiner Perkins, Menlo Ventures, 8VC, Ballistic Ventures, and In-Q-Tel participating. This is reported as a record raise for a security startup at such an early stage.

## Thesis
Armadin is not a direct FinServ play, but it matters for your thesis in two ways: first, as AI agents gain privileged access to financial systems, autonomous red-teaming becomes the only way to stay ahead of AI-powered attackers — this gives you a potential partner for a "security-by-design" narrative around the regulated infrastructure layer. Second, Mandia's credibility and the scale of the raise signals that autonomous AI security is now a consensus investment theme, meaning the compliance and safety controls you'd embed in a regulated agent infrastructure are increasingly investable as standalone categories.
