## One-liner
Crossmint provides the full agentic payments stack — agent wallets, virtual Visa/Mastercard cards, stablecoin infrastructure, verifiable credentials, and spending guardrails — enabling AI agents to transact autonomously while maintaining programmable compliance controls and a full audit trail.

## FinServ Signal
Backed by Ribbit Capital (specialist fintech VC), Franklin Templeton (major asset manager), and Lightspeed Faction in a $23.6M round. The company has shipped virtual card issuance via Visa Intelligent Platform and supports stablecoin payouts, positioning it as direct infrastructure for regulated agentic commerce. Its KYA (Know Your Agent) credential layer addresses the identity and accountability requirements that financial institutions will face as agents make autonomous purchases.

## Team
Founded by Rodrigo Acosta and Alfonso Gómez-Jordana. Investors include Ribbit Capital, Franklin Templeton, Lightspeed Faction. Funding: $23.6M raised in 2025. The Ribbit and Franklin Templeton backing is a strong signal of institutional fintech confidence.

## Thesis
As AI agents begin autonomously transacting on behalf of users and businesses, the missing piece is a compliant, auditable identity and payment layer that financial institutions can trust. Crossmint is building that layer end-to-end: agent wallets, virtual cards, stablecoin rails, and verifiable credentials that map each agent action back to a human principal. This gives you a potential infrastructure partner or acquisition target for the "trusted agent payments" thesis — the company is already wiring AI agents into Visa and Mastercard rails, which is exactly the regulated infrastructure gap your strategy is positioned to fill.
