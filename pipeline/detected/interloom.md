# Interloom
**One-liner:** Enterprise knowledge infrastructure for AI agents — captures undocumented expert decision-making ("tacit knowledge") and converts it into permanent, queryable memory that AI agents and new employees use to execute complex workflows accurately.
**Gap category:** Agent Orchestration
**Stage:** Seed (€14.2M / $16.5M, March 2026; DN Capital lead, Bek Ventures, Air Street Capital)
**Geography:** Munich, Germany
**FinServ signal:** Commerzbank (customer — reduced documentation gap from 50% to 5% on customer support emails) and Zurich Insurance both confirmed as enterprise customers.
**Team signal:** Fabian Jakobi (CEO). No high-profile prior exit or named pedigree found. DN Capital (DACH-focused growth VC) conviction is a positive institutional signal.
**Product maturity:** Live product (Commerzbank, Zurich Insurance, JLL, Volkswagen in production)
**Business model:** SaaS

## Why This Matters
Interloom solves the "why does my agent give wrong answers inside my institution" problem — a real and underappreciated blocker to regulated deployment. The Commerzbank result (50% to 5% documentation gap) is unusually concrete ROI evidence for a seed-stage company, and the finserv customer base is the best of any EU-based company in the current pipeline.

## Why You Might Pass
Knowledge infrastructure is a supporting capability, not a trust/governance layer — it makes agents smarter but doesn't give regulators the audit trail, policy enforcement, or identity assurance they require. The team signal is the lowest in the current set of new entrants, and the tacit knowledge category is not uniquely defensible without strong data network effects.
