# Sphinx

**URL:** https://www.sphinx.ai
**Gap:** Compliance
**Stage:** Seed
**Geo:** USA (New York, NY)
**Score:** 82/100

## One-liner
Sphinx deploys browser-native AI agents that operate directly inside existing compliance systems — case management platforms, third-party portals, email, and PDFs — to automate AML, KYC, and KYB investigations without requiring institutions to adopt new tooling.

## FinServ Signal
Sphinx is purpose-built for financial crime compliance. It has already handled millions of alerts and hundreds of thousands of cases in production, with a live customer base that includes Equals Money (94% reduction in false positives) and multiple internationally-operating financial institutions running compliance globally without expanding headcount. The company describes itself as "every financial institution's last compliance hire." Backed by Y Combinator and Cherry Ventures, with Deel Ventures and Singularity Capital also participating.

## Team
Co-founded by Alexandre Berkovic and Chrisjan Wüst. Backed by Cherry Ventures (lead), Y Combinator, Rebel Fund, Deel Ventures, and Singularity Capital. $7.1M seed round closed February 2026.

## Thesis
This is a near-direct fit for the compliance layer of your regulated infrastructure thesis. Sphinx operates at the workflow level inside the tools institutions already use, which positions it as a potential module in a broader compliance-grade agent stack rather than a competing platform. If you are building the regulated operating system that agents connect to, Sphinx-style AML and KYC agent automation is the kind of horizontal capability that a regulated financial infrastructure layer should either partner with or offer as a native module. The browser-native, no-new-system architecture is a significant differentiator — it removes the primary adoption barrier in compliance-heavy institutions.
