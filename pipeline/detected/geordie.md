# Geordie

**URL:** https://www.geordie.ai
**Gap:** AI Safety & Guardrails
**Stage:** Seed
**Geo:** London, UK
**Score:** 76

## One-liner
Geordie is an agent-native security platform that gives enterprises real-time discovery, behaviour monitoring, and risk control of AI agents operating across their environments.

## FinServ Signal
No named financial services customers yet, but London HQ places Geordie at the centre of the FCA-regulated market. General Catalyst (co-lead, deep finserv portfolio) provides strategic distribution into financial services. Named representative vendor in Gartner's Market Guide for Guardian Agents. Revenue grew 10× in two months with secured agents increasing 10× in five months — suggesting rapid enterprise adoption that likely includes regulated verticals.

## Team
Founded by Henry Comfort (ex-COO Americas, Darktrace), Hanah-Marie Darley (ex-Director of Security and AI Strategy, Darktrace), and Benji Weber (ex-Senior Director of Engineering, Snyk). Cybersecurity-native leadership with deep experience in enterprise-scale AI security and developer tooling. $6.5M seed co-led by Ten Eleven Ventures (specialist cybersecurity VC) and General Catalyst.

## Thesis
Geordie occupies the "guardian agent" layer — monitoring what agents do, which systems they access, and whether behaviour drifts from policy. Named "Most Innovative Startup" at RSAC 2026, the defining enterprise security conference. The platform's real-time agent discovery and behavioural risk intelligence directly addresses the governance requirements regulators are beginning to formalise (EU AI Act, FCA AI guidance, DORA). The Darktrace pedigree means the team understands how to sell AI-powered security to risk-averse enterprises, including banks. The gap between "horizontal agent governance" and "regulated-industry agent governance" is one vertical sales motion — and London is the right city to make that move.

## Why you might pass
Currently horizontal — no explicit finserv-specific compliance features (FINRA, FCA SM&CR, MiFID suitability). The thesis prefers purpose-built regulated infrastructure over general enterprise security. The AI Safety acquisition wave (Promptfoo→OpenAI, Prompt Security→SentinelOne, Lakera→Check Point) suggests horizontal agent security companies face absorption risk. Geordie would need to deepen its regulated-industry moat to avoid the same fate.
