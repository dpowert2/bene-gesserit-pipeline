Onyx Security Launches with $40M: A Secure AI Control Plane for Enterprise Agents

Onyx Security emerged from stealth on March 13 with $40M from Conviction Partners and Cyberstarts, unveiling a platform that continuously discovers AI agents, monitors each step of their reasoning, and approves or corrects agent actions in real time. Backed by a team of 70 and already used by Fortune 500 companies, Onyx uses its own supervisory agents to enforce governance policies — blocking actions, requiring human approval, or redirecting agent behaviour as needed. Regulation Reinforces

FINRA Issues Specific Guidance on AI Agent Hallucination Risk at Broker-Dealers

FINRA's 2026 Annual Regulatory Oversight Report dedicated a full standalone section to GenAI for the first time, with specific guidance on agentic AI agents that can "act beyond the user's actual or intended scope and authority." The regulator now explicitly recommends narrow permissions, audit trails, and human checkpoints before agent execution — language that maps directly to the infrastructure primitives your regulated OS needs to provide. FINRA also confirmed it holds AI-generated communications to the same standards as human-generated ones, raising the compliance bar for any firm deploying agents at the customer interface.

Agent Orchestration Reinforces

Google AP2 Protocol Gains Traction: 60+ Organisations Back Agent-Native Payments Standard

Google's Agent Payments Protocol (AP2), announced in late 2025 with 60+ backing organisations, is maturing into an industry standard for how AI agents initiate and complete financial transactions without human intervention. Together with Coinbase's x402 crypto-native payments standard, two competing paradigms are emerging for agent-native payments infrastructure. The regulated financial operating system you are building will need to take a position on which protocol(s) to support — or offer a translation layer between both.
